Welcome to ParkinAid

ParkinAid is a free, web-based diagnostic platform designed to help detect early signs of Parkinson’s disease. This diagnostic tool is developed under the non-profit organization © ParkinAid.

Powered by advanced AI models, our tool analyzes video and audio samples—such as gait impairment, hand tremors, and speech patterns—to generate a series of predicted Parkinson’s Index Scores. These index scores are standards used in clinical fields.

Our goal is to support early awareness and intervention through accessible, technology-driven insights.

User Instruction
Please follow the instructions below to record video and audio samples for each diagnostic mode
Diagnostic mode 1 - Body Motion Analysis
Purpose: This analysis captures your full-body motion as you turn in place, helping our AI assess signs of Parkinsonian movement patterns such as rigidity, freezing of gait, or balance issues.
Procedure:

1. Find a safe, well-lit space with room to turn in place.
2. Set up your camera 3–6 feet away, ensuring your full body is visible.
3. Stand still with arms relaxed at your sides.
4. Start recording, then begin turning in place at a steady, comfortable speed.
5. Continue turning for approximately 45 seconds, keeping feet mostly in the same spot.
6. Stop the recording and ensure the video is clear and stable.
7. Upload the video by navigating to the "Diagnosis" tab in the side menu and selecting the "Body Motion Analysis" feature.

Diagnostic mode 2 - Hand Tremor Analysis
Purpose: This analysis evaluates both the amplitude and frequency of hand tremors. It helps our AI detect potential Parkinsonian essential tremors.
Procedure:

1. Find a clean, well-lit space with a plain background.
2. Hold your hand outstretched and still, with fingers spread naturally.
3. Position the camera at a bottom-up side angle so all 5 fingers are clearly visible.
4. Try to keep your hand steady and relaxed without unnecessary movement.
5. Record for approximately 30 seconds, ensuring minimal background noise and visual distractions.
6. Upload the video by navigating to the "Diagnosis" tab in the side menu and selecting the "Hand Tremor Analysis" feature.

Diagnostic mode 3 - Speech Semantics Analysis
Purpose: This analysis evaluates the structure and content of your speech using part-of-speech tagging to detect linguistic patterns that may be affected by Parkinson’s disease. It focuses on grammar and semantic variety. Please note: this feature is not very accurate by far shown by experiments.
Procedure:

1. Find a quiet space with minimal background noise.
2. Position your microphone or device to clearly capture your voice.
3. Speak naturally and clearly for at least 40 seconds.
4. Describe your favorite item, activity, or experience—and explain why you like it.
5. Avoid long pauses or reading from a script; spontaneous speech is most effective.
6. Upload your audio recording by navigating to the "Diagnosis" tab in the side menu and selecting the "Speech Semantics Analysis" feature.

Diagnostic mode 4 - Speech Phonation Analysis
Purpose: This analysis evaluates the strength, steadiness, and quality of vocal fold vibration. By measuring sustained vowel sounds, the tool detects irregularities in vocal control, pitch stability, and breath support—early indicators of Parkinsonian speech changes.
Procedure:

1. Find a quiet environment with minimal background noise.
2. Position your microphone close to your mouth without touching it.
3. Take a deep breath and say “Ahhh” in a steady tone at a comfortable pitch and volume.
4. Sustain the sound for as long as possible—ideally 10–15 seconds.
5. Try to avoid pitch breaks or fluctuations.
6. Upload your audio recording by navigating to the "Diagnosis" tab and selecting the "Speech Phonation Analysis" feature.

Diagnostic mode 5 - Speech Articulation & Prosody Analysis
Purpose: This analysis evaluates both articulation—clarity and precision of speech sounds—and prosody, including rhythm, pitch variation, and expressiveness. It uses the standardized “Rainbow Passage,” a widely accepted tool in clinical and research settings, to provide a consistent and informative sample of connected speech.
Procedure:

1. Find a quiet with minimal background noise.
2. Position your microphone or device to clearly capture your voice.
3. Read the “Rainbow Passage” aloud at a comfortable pace—this should take about 40 seconds.
4. You can view or download the passage here: Standardized Reading Passages.
5. Avoid rushing or pausing unnaturally; speak as naturally as possible.
6. Upload your recording in the “Diagnosis” tab by selecting the “Speech Articulation & Prosody Analysis” feature.
